Grain Anatomy

A single grain kernel contains many beneficial vitamins and minerals (fig. 1).

Figure 1. Anatomy of a Grain Kernel

Source: GrowNYC

  • The bran, the outside layer of the kernel, constitutes 14 percent of the grain (GrowNYC 2010, 2). It is loaded with B vitamins, calcium, iron, magnesium, phosphorous, potassium, zinc, copper, manganese, selenium, dietary fiber and iron (Basey 2004; Lazor 2013, 205).
  • The germ, the embryo of the seed, makes up 2.5 percent of the kernel and contains 10 percent fat in the form of essential fatty acids (GrowNYC 2010, 2). It is rich in B vitamins, including thiamin, riboflavin, and niacin, and vitamin E (Basey 2004).
  • The endosperm (or starch), which serves as the food component for the embryo, makes up 83 percent of the kernel (Lazor 2013, 205). It has iron, protein, carbohydrates, B vitamins, and soluble fiber (GrowNYC 2010, 2). Conventional white flour consists purely of ground endosperm, whereas whole wheat flour includes varying amounts of the germ and bran.
