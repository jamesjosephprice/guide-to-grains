Buckwheat Pie Crust

Yield: 1 pie crust

½ cup + 2 tablespoons buckwheat flour

½ cup + 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

½ teaspoon salt

2 tablespoons light brown sugar

1 stick (4 ounces) unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

5 tablespoons cold water

  1. Combine buckwheat flour, all-purpose flour, salt, and brown sugar in a medium-sized bowl. Stir together.
  2. Add the butter and coat with the flour mixture. With a pastry blender, cut the butter into the flour mixture until only pea-size pieces of butter remain.
  3. Sprinkle 3 tablespoons of water over the mixture. Use the pastry blender to incorporate the water. Add remaining 2 tablespoons of water and use your hands to mix the dough until it gathers into a ball, only adding more water ‒ 1 teaspoon at a time ‒ if dry bits of dough remain. Bring the dough together and shape into a flat disc. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before using.
