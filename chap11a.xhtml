Spelt Banana Bread

Loosely adapted from Joy the Baker

Yield: 1 loaf

6 ounces unsalted butter, browned and cooled

2 cups spelt flour

½ cup light brown sugar

¼ cup granulated sugar

1 teaspoon baking soda

½ teaspoon salt

½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg

2 large eggs

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

½ cup buttermilk

3 medium bananas, mashed

2 teaspoons molasses

1-2 cups of toasted walnuts, chocolate chips, and/or toasted shredded coconut (optional)

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Prepare loaf pan with either butter and flour or pan spray, and line bottom with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In a medium bowl, beat eggs, sugars, vanilla extract, and buttermilk. Stir in the mashed bananas and molasses, then whisk in the cooled browned butter.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the bowl of dry ingredients, stir altogether. Throw in walnuts, chocolate chips, and/or toasted shredded coconut and give the batter a quick stir.
  5. Pour batter into prepared loaf pan. Bake bread until a toothpick inserted in center comes out clean, about 60 minutes. Cool the loaf in the pan on a rack for 15 minutes, then remove loaf from pan, and cool completely on rack.
